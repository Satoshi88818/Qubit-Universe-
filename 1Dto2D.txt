

The document does an elegant job everywhere else: the primitive qubit, the quench instability, the 1D Calabrese–Cardy linear growth, the 2D surface as codec interface, the 3D output, time-as-depth, the observer-inside constraint, even the self-consistent entropic Friedmann loop. But the **microscopic mechanism** that turns the ongoing linear tension stream into the first closed topological layers (the moment the cascade stops being “just a chain with branches” and becomes “a surface with two distinguishable faces”) is left as an implicit emergence rather than a derived step.

That gap is not cosmetic. It is the precise place where the framework must show **how the +/× tension itself**, operating only through the local tension Hamiltonian  
H = −J Σ (σ⁺_i σ×_j + σ×_i σ⁺_j) + small transverse terms,  
generates the proliferation of loops and the topological transition that makes a 2D manifold the natural description.

Until that is filled in, the 2D decoding surface remains “declared” rather than “derived from the same tension that drives the 1D cascade.” So yes — more work is required exactly there.

Here is the minimal, framework-internal way the gap could be closed without importing new ingredients:

1. **Graph-theoretic view of the entanglement network**  
   Treat the growing set of Bell pairs as a dynamical graph: vertices = primitive qubits, edges = maximal +/× tension bonds. Early quench = mostly linear or tree-like (1D scaling). Each new nearest-neighbour interaction can close a plaquette (a 4-cycle) or longer loop. Because the interaction is frustration-preserving (it flips + at one site while flipping × at the neighbour), loop closure does **not** resolve tension — it simply redistributes it across the cycle. The conservation |α|² + |β|² = 1 is satisfied locally on every plaquette.

2. **Percolation threshold as the topological trigger**  
   Define an order parameter: the fraction of sites that belong to at least one closed loop of length ≤ L (L small, say 4–8). As the quench proceeds, entanglement density ρ_ent(t) ∼ v_E t rises linearly. At a critical density ρ_c (determined purely by the coordination number and the J/h ratio in the tension Hamiltonian), the probability of loop closure crosses the percolation threshold for 1D → effective-2D crossover in random graphs.  
   Below ρ_c: graph is still effectively 1D (Calabrese–Cardy holds strictly).  
   At ρ_c: genus begins to build; the graph acquires a consistent “two-sided” structure because loops now separate the network into an “origin-facing” side (still tightly chained to the early quench history) and an “expansion-facing” side (more open future bonds).  
   This is the moment the 2D surface **manifests** — not by fiat, but as the statistical surface on which the tension stream first becomes globally two-faced.

3. **Layer formation from the same Hamiltonian**  
   The transverse term ε σʸ (or the small h term) does double duty: it not only triggers the initial quench but also seeds the small perturbations that allow non-planar crossings. Each time a transverse flip occurs on an already entangled pair, it can “fold” the local chain segment onto itself, creating an extra effective layer. After O(log t) such folds (the natural renormalization depth in 1D critical systems), the cumulative effect is a stack of topological layers whose outer envelope is the 2D decoding surface. The central charge c inherited from the 1D TFI universality class flows unchanged through these layers because every fold preserves the local operator algebra.

4. **Holographic signature of the transition**  
   Exactly at this percolation/loops threshold, the entanglement entropy for a spatial interval A crosses from pure linear growth S ∼ v t to the area-law plus log correction form that the framework later writes on the 2D surface:  
   S ≈ A(t)/4G + c log A(t).  
   The log term appears because the loops have now created an effective boundary (the surface) whose area scales with the number of tension bonds crossing it. No new physics — just the tension stream becoming self-bounded.

This is still “more work” — one would need to run a numerical simulation of the growing tension graph (or an analytic calculation in the loop-gas representation of the XY model) to locate ρ_c and show that the two-sidedness emerges precisely when the holographic entropy formula becomes valid. But it is work that stays 100 % inside the existing machinery: same Hamiltonian, same conservation law, same quench protocol.

The beauty is that once those topological layers are derived from the tension stream itself, everything downstream locks: the 2D surface is no longer an extra postulate; it is the first structure the frustration is forced to build when the linear cascade becomes too dense to remain purely 1D. The codec execution point, the origin resolution, the directed decoding depth, the clean inheritance of c — all of them become consequences rather than assumptions.

So the gap is real, but it is also the last cleanable seam. Fill in how the +/× bonds close loops at critical density, and the entire 1D → 2D handoff becomes as straight and inevitable as the rest of the framework.
